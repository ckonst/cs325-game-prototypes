The Halfway Child

  • Originality: The game uses original design and movement mechanics, original sound and music, as well as an original story told through gameplay.
  • Technical Merit: The jumping system utilizes nonlinear vertical acceleration, platforming created with tilemaps, animations, parallax backgrounds, and state switching.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? I learned about the difficulties of generating collision tilemaps(I couldn't get it working how I wanted) and animations (also couldn't get working quite how I wanted)
  • The easiest part was the basic mechanic (platforming) and loading in assets. The hardest parts were the animations and collision tilemapping.

  • High Concept: Help the lost girl find her way home in this platformer adventure.
  • Theme: Person who is lost.
  • Mandated Variety:
  • Inputs: mouse click on the play button at the start.
  • Keys: a,d,left,right,space for movement and jumping
  • Randomness: deterministic
  • Play styles: Explorer, Storyteller, Achiever
  • Genres: Platformer, Side-scroller
  • Prototype Goal: To test jumping, movement, shooting projectiles, hit detection, damage systems, enemy movement.
  • Player Experience Goals: Hopefully the player will be drawn into the world and its protagonist, and will be challenged by the platforming required to complete the game.
  • Gameplay: Player Actions:
  • Movement: WASD or Arrow keys
  • Jump: Spacebar
  • In the main menu use mouse to click the play button. Get to the end of the level to win the game.
  • Strategies: If you are looking to beat the game quickly don't take the high path, it's much harder. If you want a challenge, go for it, take your time as well.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: The titular Halfway girl wakes up in an unknown forest. Faced with an almost impossible task, she tries to find her way home.
  • Target Audience: People with a strong sense of aesthetic and atmosphere, and those who like platformers.
  • Play Time: 1-2 minutes.
